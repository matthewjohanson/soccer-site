The Ultimate Guide to Understanding the Offside Rule

By Coach David | Published on October 26, 2023 | In Rules & Strategy

It's the rule that causes the most confusion for new fans, parents, and even players. A perfectly good-looking goal is suddenly disallowed, and nobody seems to know why. Don't worry, we're here to break down the offside rule into simple, easy-to-understand terms.

What is Offside, Simply Put?

At its core, the offside rule prevents "goal-hanging" or "cherry-picking." It stops attacking players from simply waiting near the opponent's goal for an easy pass. A player is in an offside position if they are nearer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-to-last opponent when the ball is played to them.

Key Takeaway: You can't be behind the defenders when your teammate kicks the ball forward to you.

However, just being in an offside position is not an offense. An offense only occurs when that player becomes actively involved in the play.

The Two Conditions for an Offside Offense

For the referee to blow the whistle, two things must happen at the moment the ball is played by a teammate:

  1. The Position: The player must be in an offside position (as described above).
  2. The Action: The player must become involved in active play by interfering with play, interfering with an opponent, or gaining an advantage by being in that position.

Remember, you cannot be offside in your own half of the field, or from a throw-in, goal kick, or corner kick. For more detailed scenarios, check out this great book on soccer rules (this is an example of an affiliate link).
